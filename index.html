Web Security

How would you advise a potential client to secure his/her website from web security attacks? Note: the word count for your answer should not be greater than 250 words

There are a lot of different safety precautions to take when ensuring your website stays secure. It is not unusual to see passwords, email addresses, and bank details leaked through various websites. It is your responsibility, as a developer, to keep yourself and your users safe.

The best place to start protecting your website is to address the most common risks first and keep yourself updated on what those may be. You may lower the chances of leaked passwords by setting policies for creating and renewing passwords. Or by implementing automatic user timeout, assuring the users credentials are not left unnecessarily long in the browser.

Automatic solutions have become common practice, making less room for human error. For example, scanners help you seek out vulnerabilities and other issues in your website.

Other recommended Web security practices include; Sanitization of data, making sure the data accepted by your system is in the right format. This is either done manually or through third-party solutions like NodeJS or sanitize-html. And CI/CD tools, giving you an automatic process monitoring changes and catching oversights from developers. It is also important to be wary of where you chose to host your website, to avoid attacks through the hosting platform. You should always keep your website up to date, and perform regular backups of your website. It is better to be safe than sorry.
